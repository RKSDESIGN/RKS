Through the Lens: Moments Preserved

Artistic Philosophy

I remember as a kid, my father had a collection of National Georgraphic magazines that were kept on a book shelf. Every time I would open one I was taken to a new and breathtaking far away place with different cultures and landscape. It probably isn't a coincidence then I “went to the woods” to experience, learn and explore. It was the beginning of my journey to “see” nature. Nature has been a vehicle of artists during the ages to express themselves. The landscape genre investigates painting's ability to record the authenticity of the real world and to communicate the artist’s experiences in nature As a photographer, my great passion is to share images that expressively and visually inspire people and help connect them to the power of natural and cultural landscapes that surround us. My highest incentive is to encourage others to find paths least taken and explore our world to understand it's exposure to human influence. Through my images, I hope that others may be motivated to find places that I have been fortunate enough to call upon or seek out their own special places and thereby discover their own natural awareness and perspectives. Without awareness and perspective, how will we ever know what might be lost.
